THE INVISIBLE WORKFORCE
Casual workers.
Part-time workers.
Carers.
Invisible labour.

Women are disproportionately affected by precarious employment including casual labour, insecure contracts, and unpaid caring responsibilities.

These labour structures contribute to lower wages, reduced job security, and long-term financial instability.

Lower Income

Precarious work often results in unstable income and reduced economic security.

Job Insecurity

Casual employment structures create uncertainty and vulnerability within labour systems.

Care Burden

Women continue to carry disproportionate unpaid caring responsibilities.

Structural Inequality

Workplace equality initiatives remain unevenly accessible across industries.

THE STRUCTURAL GAP

Equality Is Not Equally Accessible.

Our research identified a structural mismatch between existing workplace equity programs and the workers most affected by precarious employment.


Grace Papers primarily operates through employer partnerships and workplace advisory systems. However, workers outside formal employment structures are often the least likely to access these forms of support.

OUR INTERVENTION
Beyond Corporate Equality.

Expand Accessibility

Increase access through more community-based support systems.

Broaden Partnerships

Collaborate with NGOs, unions, and community organisations.

Public Advocacy

Advocate for stronger labour protections and gender-responsive reforms.

Structural Change

Address wider cultural and social systems shaping labour inequality.

“Gender inequality in precarious work cannot be solved through individual resilience alone.”
